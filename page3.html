Hydrogen Isotopes

Heavy Water

Water is H20. Two hydrogen atoms for every oxygen atom. The hydrogen atom has a proton in its center. If the hydrogen has a proton and a neutron in its center it is called deuterium. Heavy water is H20, just that both hydrogens have a nucleus made up of a proton and neutron.

These hydrogen atoms are called Isotopes. The same element, with the same number of protons but with different numbers of neutrons.

Most hydrogen has no neutron in its nucleus. Hydrogen with a neutron/proton center is called deuterium.

Hydrogen with two neutrons and a proton in its center is called tritium.

Deuterium and Tritium will fuel the Tokamak.
