Hydrogen occurs naturally in 3 forms

Or Isotopes

Hydrogen is the most common element with a proton in its nucleus.

If the hydrogen has a proton and a neutron in its center it is called Deuterium. Much rarer with only 1 for every 6000 Hydrogen atoms.

The same element, with the same number of protons but with different numbers of neutrons.

Tritium is the isotope of hydrogen vital to fuel the Tokamak. The Deutrium and Tritium smash together to make Helium and realise huge amounts of energy.

Deuterium and Tritium will fuel the Tokamak.
